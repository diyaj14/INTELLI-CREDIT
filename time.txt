Lore
Level 0 - The Incident
The event took place inside the Research Lab Main Hall, (parallel to the Main Courtyard). This hall is the central experimental chamber of the Ouroboros Institute of Temporal Mechanics, a facility that operates as part of a larger community of institutes researching time travel within controlled limits.

Other institutions had already managed short-range time transfers — sending objects a few minutes into the past or future, sometimes close to one hour. However, by official definition, a Timebox is a system that can send a physical object at least one and a half hours forward or backward in time in a stable and repeatable way. Until this activation, the Ouroboros Institute had not achieved that level.

Under the supervision of Senior Researcher Dr. Elias Novikov, Version 1 of the prototype entered its final testing phase. Years of research and calibration led to this moment — an attempt to move something more than ninety minutes through time.

The chamber activated smoothly. The containment field stabilized. The monitoring systems synchronized.

Then, an unexpected displacement event was recorded.

When the containment field settled, an object had appeared inside the chamber.

It was a blade.

The metal was clearly bloodstained, with dark streaks along the edge and the handle. Near its base, an engraved timestamp showed that it had come from two hours in the future. This exceeded all previous limits and officially qualified as a successful Timebox result.

Initial checks showed that the chamber systems could not identify whose blood it was through basic surface analysis.

Dr. Novikov carefully reviewed the data before speaking to his assistant, Dr. Mira Hein. He told her to secure the blade and begin a full investigation to find out the origins of this object. He also said he needed to handle system diagnostics and recalibration elsewhere.

He stressed that the discovery must remain confidential until properly verified, as news of such a breakthrough could have serious consequences for the wider temporal research community if released too early.

With that, the responsibility was handed over.

The investigation begins.



Level 1 - Blood Analysis
Dr. Mira Hein transported the blade to the Biological Analysis Wing for detailed examination. The laboratory contained standard hematology tools, genetic sequencing systems, and contamination filtration equipment required for sample processing.

Initial testing revealed that the biological traces on the blade were heavily degraded and mixed with environmental contaminants. Multiple purification cycles were required before reliable data could be extracted.

After completing the isolation process, the sample was classified as:

Blood Type: AB negative

Further genetic sequencing was conducted to determine the source of the material. The extracted DNA markers were compared against the institute’s internal personnel data.

Result: Match found within facility personnel registry.

The analysis confirms that the biological material belongs to someone registered inside the institute.

With the identity narrowed through genetic confirmation, the information must now be cross-checked against official personnel files and documented records to determine the corresponding individual.

She pauses, uneasy about what the next confirmation might reveal.

Dr. Hein secured the blade and the analytical report, preparing to review institutional documentation to continue the investigation.


Level 2 - The Archive
With the genetic match confirmed, Dr. Mira Hein accessed the institute’s personnel database to cross-reference the extracted markers with official documentation.
The search was restricted to active staff members whose profiles matched the identified genetic data.
After processing the query, the system returned a single entry.
Name: Dr. Elias Novikov
Age: 47
Nationality: Registered as Russian Federation
Position: Senior Researcher
Department: Temporal Systems — Innovation & Development Division
Employee ID: OTI-TSD-004
Clearance Level: Maximum
The match appears on screen.
For a brief moment, Dr. Hein hesitates — silently hoping the result corresponds to someone else within the facility.
The data confirms otherwise.
Professional Record:

— Submitted multiple experimental proposals over the years
— Proposals formally reviewed and rejected by institutional oversight committees
Administrative Data:

The data confirms that the biological material recovered from the blade belongs to the Senior Researcher.
Personnel records establish identity — but they do not explain intent or context.
Further insight may be found by reviewing his experimental documentation, prototype notes, and development files stored within his innovation workspace, where active research projects are tested and refined.
Dr. Hein closes the profile and exhales slowly.
Her fear was correct.
If I want answers, I need to look into his work now.


Level 3 - The Architect
Dr. Mira Hein entered Novikov’s workspace and let the door slide shut behind her.

The systems were still running in low power, simulations paused mid-render as though he had stepped away only moments ago. Several proposal drafts remained open on the display - some marked rejected, others half-revised, waiting for submission.

She scans through them slowly.

The comments are procedural. Risk assessment too high. Institutional benefit too low. Prior discovery overlaps with existing literature. Nothing personal. Nothing humiliating.

Just dismissal.

The activity logs show something else — he had been running repeated simulations on the same divergence cluster within the temporal model. Not expanding outward like most researchers do. Not chasing grand breakthroughs.

Refining one section.
Returning to it.
Adjusting it.
Again. And again.

This doesn’t look like an obsession.

It looks like someone trying to get something right.

She leans back slightly, arms folding.

So what’s the motive?

His work wasn’t revolutionary. It wasn’t threatening. It wasn’t even particularly successful. Some of his claims had already been documented by others years before. The rest had been deemed too risky for minimal reward.

He sounds like a man losing relevance.
Not gaining power.
Not uncovering something explosive.

Losing relevance.

Her eyes drift back to the central visualization on the screen. The branching structure spreads outward in layered symmetry, but the simulation marker remains close to its core. Every iteration curves back toward that anchored origin point.

He never strayed far from it.
Why?
If someone wanted him dead… for what?
Professional rivalry? Over this?
No.
Sabotage? Over incremental recalculations?
That makes even less sense.

You don’t eliminate a man for trying and failing.

You don’t silence someone who isn’t being heard in the first place.

She exhales slowly.

Unless he wasn’t failing.

The thought lingers longer than she wants it to.

He wasn’t exploring the outer limbs of the model. He wasn’t chasing distant probabilities.

He kept circling something near the center.

Something close to the origin.

Her gaze lifts instinctively, as if she can see through the walls of the facility to the massive structure standing at its heart — rooted, branching upward and outward, the physical embodiment of everything they simulate on these screens.

He kept his research near that.

Near its base.

I stare at the display again, a quiet unease settling in.

If this was just desperation, it’s a pathetic kind. A man trying to matter.

But if it wasn’t…
Then why kill him?
Why silence someone who looks like he had already been forgotten?

That’s what doesn’t make sense.
And that’s what scares me.


Level 4 - The Anomaly
I run into the room where the timeline visualization system is active.

This is not the real timeline tree. It is a model — a projection that represents how our timeline moves and branches so we can understand it. The system marks our position clearly, showing where we stand on the main timeline inside one specific branch that continues after the Timebox activation.

I zoom into that branch and trace its path carefully. At first it moves forward like a normal timeline. A straight progression into the future with minor divergences along the way.

Then I see something wrong.

The branch bends.
Instead of continuing forward, it curves back toward an earlier point on the same timeline.

The branch intersects its own past. 

A timeline is supposed to split and move forward — not reconnect to itself.

But here the line loops through a time moment that already exists and then continues forward again.

An event from the future feeds into its own past. 

The weapon appears in the future, travels backward, and causes the death. That death becomes the condition that allows the weapon to exist and travel back again. The system repeats this sequence every time the loop completes.

Cause and effect are locked inside a closed circle.

From the outside, time appears normal. It moves forward. People live. Events unfold.

But inside this branch, one segment is permanently looping through the same time point. The repetition is not random — it is structurally embedded into the timeline model itself.

I attempt to remove the trigger in the simulation.

The system recalculates.

The model compensates.

An alternate path forms — different variables, different conditions — but the curve still reconnects to the same outcome.

 The loop persists regardless of interference.

It is not dependent on a single object or a single action. It is stabilized by the structure of the branch. Any attempt to remove it creates a replacement path that preserves the cycle.

This is only a visualization of the timeline — but it reflects the actual temporal structure.

And if the model is accurate, then this branch cannot exist without the loop completing.

I cannot break the loop without collapsing the branch itself.

If the branch collapses, the timeline tied to it may destabilize.

If it destabilizes, parts of our present may disappear.

For this timeline to continue existing, the loop must finish.

For it to stop, the entire sequence must be erased.

As I force myself to step away from the projection, I notice something on the console.

I find a torn sheet of paper tucked under the timeline tree console, half-hidden in the space beneath the desk. It looks discarded, like something that was handled quickly and then pushed aside. The edges are uneven, as if it was separated from a larger document in haste.

 This paper is not random.

Most of the visible text is faded and smudged, and several sections are crossed out or obscured. The layout, however, follows a structured format that resembles a system-generated record rather than a handwritten note. Even though this copy is damaged, the formatting and coded entries suggest that it was originally produced through a centralized system.

The document feels incomplete — like a duplicate that can be reproduced from its source. A clearer version likely still exists where it was first generated.

And as I stand there staring at the glowing curve that feeds into itself, one realization settles over me with terrifying clarity —

The murder is not avoidable.
It is already woven into the structure of time


Level 5 - The Author
I find a sheet under the store counter — an inventory record. The top shows a project name: Project Ouroboros. Below it is a list of items. Every object I have encountered since the beginning of this investigation appears on it — the syringe, the documents, the printed records, the monitoring tools, the materials tied to the timeline system. They are not random supplies. They are recorded as purchased components under one coordinated batch.

Was this about control from the beginning? Were these things never clues but parts of something already prepared? If everything was purchased under one plan, then my investigation was not discovery — it was execution. I walked through the steps he designed, thinking I was uncovering truth, but I was only activating what was already written.

The document shows authorization marks. His name appears as the primary approver. There is also a secondary confirmation line that aligns with my role. The timestamps indicate that these purchases were made before key events unfolded — before the investigation truly began.

So the sequence of actions was established in advance. He bought the materials first. Then the events happened. Then I followed the path. My decisions were not outside the structure — they were anticipated and positioned inside it.

I look at the project title again.

Project Ouroboros.

It reads like a structural experiment built to sustain repetition. The items listed are not isolated tools. They are interlocking components meant to trigger a controlled event that returns to its origin.

He did not create a failed theory. He created a mechanism. A system that survives through repetition. The loop resets through the weapon — which means the purpose of this project was never discovery. It was continuity.

And continuity was never meant to save anyone.

This was not built for the greater good. Not to stabilize time. Not to protect reality. It was built so that he would stand alone.

Not praised. Not celebrated. Beyond that. Untouchable.

If the loop cannot break, then neither can his achievement. If the event repeats forever, then the mind that engineered it stands above every other mind that ever studied time.

He did not want recognition from them. He wanted transcendence over them.

He did not want to be acknowledged by the temporal community. He wanted to eclipse it..

The roles become clear in my mind.

He is THE ARCHITECT.

He defined the structure. He designed the paradox. He established the conditions under which time folds back onto itself.

And through it, he ensured something that no paper, no award, no institution could ever grant him — permanence of dominance.

If the loop never ends, then neither does the moment of his death. And if that moment is eternal, then so is he. He will die for eternity — and therefore, in a twisted sense, he will also live for eternity.

Not remembered. Repeated.

Immortality not through survival, but through inevitability.

I am THE AUTHOR.

I executed the sequence. I collected the materials. I followed the path. I activated the conditions embedded inside the system.

The weapon functions as the control element — the mechanism that forces the event back through time and guarantees that the loop restarts every time it completes.

Three parts. One system. Designer. Executor. Controller. This is not accidental tragedy — it is engineered inevitability.

I fold the sheet slowly.

There is no confusion left inside me now.

Only understanding.

He built eternity for himself.

The only way forward is to return to him…
to complete what the structure requires.
